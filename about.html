Background

I am a cybersecurity researcher and practitioner with nearly 20 years of hands-on experience in electronics and industrial systems. My work sits at the intersection of physical-layer security and critical infrastructure defense — approaching ICS/OT security from the sensor up, not just the network down.

As the author of the "Inputs Lie" research framework, I investigate how deterministic logic in SCADA and ICS environments can be manipulated through physical-layer signal drift, GNSS timing manipulation, and unverified external inputs. My research addresses a structural blind spot in current security frameworks: critical infrastructure systems implicitly trust external signals that can be manipulated below traditional detection thresholds by patient, sophisticated adversaries.

Beyond research, I have served as a technical lead for Identity and Access Management programs in regulated financial and critical infrastructure-adjacent environments, bridging the gap between security operations and policy compliance.

Core Expertise

Physical-Layer Security

GNSS/GPS timing vulnerabilities, RF signal integrity, and low signal-to-noise ratio attack detection in ICS/OT environments. Understanding how attacks live below the threshold of conventional monitoring.

Hardware Assurance

20 years of diagnostic experience with electronic systems, control circuits, and industrial protocols including Modbus and DNP3. Hardware-first thinking applied to security architecture.

ICS/OT Security Frameworks

Applied practitioner across MITRE ATT&CK for ICS, NERC CIP, NIST 800-82, and the Risk Management Framework (RMF) in regulated critical infrastructure environments.

Identity & Access Management

Technical lead for IAM programs in regulated financial and critical infrastructure-adjacent environments, with emphasis on privileged access and zero-trust architecture.

Research & Community

I am an active contributor to the ICS security community, presenting research on the convergence of SIGINT, satellite cybersecurity, and ICS/OT security. I presented "When Cyber Meets the Spectrum" at BSides Delaware and serve as a long-term organizer and volunteer.

Current research targets conference submission to the ICS Cyber Security Conference and S4, focusing on GNSS timing manipulation as an attack vector against critical infrastructure control systems.
