Critical Infrastructure: What It Is & Why You Should Care

Norris Cornell · Foundational · Critical Infrastructure · Public Awareness
Imagine waking up without electricity, running water, or a way to call for help.

That's the chaos we'd face without critical infrastructure — the essential systems, assets, and networks that keep society running. Energy, water, transportation, healthcare, telecommunications. They're so vital that their disruption can cripple national security, economic stability, public health, or safety. They're the backbone of daily life — and their protection matters far more than most people realize.

We rely on these systems every moment of every day — almost always without noticing. To understand what's actually at stake, let's walk through a typical day and see how many of them touch your life before you've even had lunch.

How Critical Infrastructure Powers Your Day

6:00 AM

Morning Kickoff

By the time you're sipping your first coffee, multiple sectors are already working on your behalf. The lights came on because the grid is running. The water pressure in your shower and the tap you brushed your teeth under exists because a treatment plant and pumping infrastructure operated overnight without incident.

ENERGY SECTOR → WATER SECTOR
8:00 AM

The Commute

The roads, bridges, and traffic signals you navigate are maintained by the Transportation Sector. The GPS directions guiding you, the traffic update you heard on the radio, the quick call you made en route — all of it runs on Communications Sector infrastructure that most people assume is simply "the internet."

TRANSPORTATION SECTOR → COMMUNICATIONS
9:00 AM

At Work

Checking email, pulling up documents, connecting to colleagues across the country — that's the Information Technology Sector. The building you're sitting in, whether office, retail space, or facility, falls under the Commercial Facilities Sector, which includes physical security, access control, and the HVAC keeping you comfortable.

IT SECTOR → COMMERCIAL FACILITIES
12:00 PM

Lunch Break

The sandwich you grabbed exists because the Food and Agriculture Sector handled production, processing, and distribution — a supply chain spanning farms, processing facilities, cold storage, and delivery logistics. The tap to pay for it ran through the Financial Services Sector: banking systems, payment rails, and fraud detection all operating in the background of a two-second transaction.

FOOD & AGRICULTURE → FINANCIAL SERVICES
3:00 PM

Health Check

A visit to a doctor or pharmacy touches the Healthcare and Public Health Sector — hospitals, clinics, pharmacies, and the supply chains that keep medications available. Behind every emergency call, fire truck, and ambulance crew is the Emergency Services Sector, standing by so that when things go wrong, there's someone to call.

EMERGENCY SERVICES → HEALTHCARE
9:00 PM

Evening Wind-Down

Streaming a movie, scrolling your phone, adjusting the thermostat — the Communications Sector is still running your connectivity, and the Energy Sector is still powering your TV, HVAC, and lights. From the moment you woke up to the moment you go to sleep, critical infrastructure was running continuously in every corner of your day.

COMMUNICATIONS SECTOR → ENERGY SECTOR

Why Critical Infrastructure Security Matters

These sectors hum in the background, enabling the routines we take for granted. But what happens when they fail? Disruptions aren't just inconvenient — they're dangerous, threatening lives, livelihoods, and entire communities. Two examples make this concrete.

CASE · FEBRUARY 2021

Texas Power Crisis

A severe winter storm caused cascading failures across the Texas power grid, leaving millions of residents without electricity — in freezing temperatures — for days. Water pipes froze and burst. Hospitals struggled. Over 240 people died.

The crisis wasn't caused by a cyberattack. It was caused by infrastructure that wasn't hardened for conditions that, in hindsight, were predictable. The lesson is the same: dependency on critical systems without investment in their resilience is a risk that eventually materializes.

~4.5 million households lost power · 246+ deaths · $195B in damages
CASE · MAY 2017

WannaCry Ransomware

The WannaCry ransomware attack infected over 200,000 systems across 150 countries in a single day. Among the hardest hit: the UK's National Health Service, which was forced to cancel thousands of appointments and turn away non-critical patients when hospital computers locked up mid-operation.

The attack exploited an unpatched Windows vulnerability — the same basic patch management failure discussed in every cyber hygiene guide ever written. Basic hygiene failures in critical systems have consequences that extend far beyond the IT department.

200,000+ systems affected · 150 countries · NHS forced to divert emergency care

These aren't distant scenarios. They happened recently, in modern developed countries with significant resources — and they were largely preventable.

Are We Prepared for a Crisis?

Critical infrastructure isn't glamorous. It doesn't generate headlines until something goes wrong — and by then, the cost is already being paid by real people. The power that didn't come on. The call that didn't go through. The medication that wasn't available.

Understanding what critical infrastructure is and how it works is the first step toward having an informed perspective on how it should be protected. Security professionals, policy makers, engineers, and ordinary citizens all have a role — even if that role is simply understanding what's at stake when we make decisions about investment, maintenance, and risk tolerance.

Next time your day runs smoothly, give it a quiet nod — and ask yourself: how prepared are we if one of these lifelines goes down?

That question is where this field starts. Every post on this site is an attempt to help answer it.
