Inputs Lie: The Structural Blind Spot in ICS Trust Models

FRAMEWORK

SCADA and ICS environments are built on deterministic logic — if an input signal says a value, the system acts on it. But what happens when that input is wrong? Not wrong due to a fault, but wrong due to deliberate manipulation, operating below the threshold of any installed monitoring system?

The "Inputs Lie" framework addresses a structural blind spot: critical infrastructure systems implicitly trust external signals — GNSS timing, sensor feeds, industrial protocol inputs — that can be manipulated by patient, sophisticated adversaries using physical-layer techniques. GNSS spoofing, signal injection, and sub-threshold drift are not theoretical. They are the next frontier of attacks against infrastructure that assumes its inputs are clean.

When the Grid Becomes a Targeting Sensor: The Lethal Feedback Loop

WHITE PAPER

Smart grid infrastructure — built to optimize energy distribution — generates continuous, high-resolution data about occupancy patterns, operational rhythms, and activity signatures at facilities across the national grid. This paper examines how that telemetry can be repurposed as a passive intelligence-collection layer, creating a feedback loop in which the infrastructure being protected becomes the instrument of targeting.

The implications for critical infrastructure operators, defense planners, and civilian facilities connected to smart grid networks represent an underexamined convergence of physical security and cyber intelligence.
